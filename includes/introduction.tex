Rapid technological advancement in computing power and data storage has enabled
the \gls{ml} field to evolve significantly. This evolution has led to the development
of versatile \gls{ml} models applied across many domains.\ \glspl{llm} have emerged
as one of the most popular types of \gls{ml} models, demonstrating various and
widespread practical applications.
\newline

The natural language interface of \glspl{llm} makes them valuable as assistants
in various tasks. A key feature is their ability to convert natural language
instructions into executable commands. The \gls{cli} is one field where this
capability can enhance user experience and \gls{qol} for developers and regular
users alike. The \gls{cli} remains an essential part of modern development
processes, enabling quick and efficient task execution.
\newline

For most such solutions, the \gls{llm} is accessed remotely via an API, which
introduces limitations such as latency, dependency on network connectivity, and
potential privacy concerns. Running \glspl{llm} locally on consumer hardware
offers a promising alternative, providing low-latency, offline assistance and
full control over the software.

This motivates us to explore and evaluate different approaches for building
a Linux \gls{cli} assistant powered by local \glspl{llm}. The work investigates multiple
possible applications of \glspl{llm} for \gls{cli} assistance, evaluating their
practicality and performance. Through implementation and testing, we will assess
which approaches are practical for offline, resource-constrained environments and which prove
viable for real-world usage.
