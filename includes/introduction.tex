Rapid technological advancement in computing power and data storage has enabled
the \gls{ml} field to evolve significantly, leading to the development of
versatile models applied across many domains. \Glspl{llm} have emerged as one of
the most popular types, demonstrating widespread practical applications.

They can significantly enhance the \gls{cli} user experience and
provide a better \gls{qol} for developers and everyday users. Because the
\gls{cli} remains essential for efficient development, integrating \glspl{llm}
allows users to interact via natural language, creating a simpler interface and
making them highly valuable assistants for terminal-based tasks.

One major drawback of current solutions is that \glspl{llm} are accessed
remotely on a server, introducing latency, dependency on network connectivity,
and privacy concerns. In contrast, running \glspl{llm} locally on consumer
hardware offers a promising alternative, providing low-latency, offline access
and full control over data.

This motivates us to explore and evaluate different approaches for building a
Linux \gls{cli} assistant powered by local \glspl{llm}. The work investigates
multiple applications of \glspl{llm} for \gls{cli} assistance, evaluating their
practicality and performance through implementation and testing to assess which
approaches prove viable for real-world usage.

The structure of this thesis is as follows:

Section 1 provides the necessary theoretical background for understanding the
field of the thesis, concepts of technologies used, and the rationale behind the
implementation of the tools we will be using.

Section 2 provides a comprehensive review of existing solutions, tools and
applications that are relevant to the evolution of the solution and scope of the
thesis.

Section 3 provides a description of the architectural design, implementation
details, and describes the considerations and decisions made during the design
and implementation of the solution.

Section 4 outlines the evaluation methodology for the solution. The
evaluation is structured to assess system components, with particular focus on
what worked well and what proved impractical.

In Section 6, we discuss the results of the evaluation, and provide insights
on the implications of the results, and discuss the opportunities for future
work.

Conclusion section summarizes the key findings of the thesis, and provides final
thoughts on the research conducted.
